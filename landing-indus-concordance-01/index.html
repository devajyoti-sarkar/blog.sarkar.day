FAQ

What is the Indus Script?

The Indus Civilization was the largest of the four civilizations of the ancient world – Egypt, Mesopotamia, China and the Indus. At its peak, it was the size of the modern European Union. Unfortunately, the Indus Script is the last great script to remain undeciphered. It survives in inscriptions found on seals, sealings, pottery and other objects. Until its decipherment, the Indus Civilization will remain a mystery of prehistory.

Why have another Concordance?

While there are a limited number of resources that can serve as a concordance for the Indus script, this Concordance is unique because it is based on a full decipherment of the script that will be published separately. It is the first to be able to parse inscriptions into constituent words, patronymics and other key linguistic components based on an actual reading. It is best able to provide the kind of reference required to support decipherment and other efforts.

Is it comprehensive?

Alas no. It compiles a corpus based on the first four volumes of the Corpus of Indus seals and Inscriptions that cover about 3,649 objects with 5,037 inscriptions from across 40 Indus sites. A future work will include other seals that are available in ASI journals of the relevant digs as well as future books of the CISI series. But it unquestionably covers the significant majority of seals discovered so far.

What other resources will be a part of this work?

This book is a part of the output of a personal project to decipher the Indus script. The primary goal of this effort is to publish a full decipherment of the Indus script. Separate to this we will publish a number of works on the Rig Veda which include original translations of key Suktas required to understand this decipherment. We will publish a number of research papers including a proposal for the Unicode Encoding of the Indus Script. We are releasing the Ida font which is a commercial grade, open licensed font for the Indus Script that was created as a part of this effort. You can follow us on our blog.
